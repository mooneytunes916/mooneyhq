My goal is to make the people I work with better at the work they're already trying to do. That looks like leading a team well. It also looks like building the tool the team didn't know they needed.

I go by Mooney. Always have. Working on making it the legal version too.

I spent close to a decade running operations the old-fashioned way: boots on the ground, P&Ls in spreadsheets, problems handled with a phone call. Then I met a tool that could do the spreadsheet part for me. Now I lead a 10,000-vehicle rental fleet at Lyft, build the AI systems that take the repetitive work off my team's plate, and run a couple of small businesses on the side. The fun part is the same as it ever was: figure out the hard thing, put the right people and pieces in place, and watch it run.

I love the gap between operations and engineering. A small team that understands both can ship things a large org full of specialists can't. I enjoy mentoring people on the field operations side who want to learn how to build, and I'm at my best when I'm close enough to the actual problem to design the solution myself.

Outside of work I'm training for a HYROX race, walking two large dogs, writing custom songs for kids who won't put their shoes on, and slowly bringing a 2005 BMW back to life. I live in Sacramento.
